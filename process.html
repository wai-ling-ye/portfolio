The Design Process

Executing a design driven by a process allows me to consistently produce high quality results. My process can be divided into the following stages:

  1. Discovery and Strategy

  2. Information Architecture

  3. Logo and Branding

  4. Visual Design

Each step of the process builds on the previous step, ensuring that early discoveries and user observances are incorporated into every iteration of the design.

Phase 1: Discovery and Strategy

The first phase focuses on first expanding on the goals and potential features of the product, analyzing the existing competitors, and discovering what need or want. Then, based on the research, I narrow down the target audience and identify the differentiating features that will make our product stand out in the marketplace.

Competitive Analysis

By performing a SWOT analysis on a variety of competitors, we can position our product to take advantage of gaps in the marketplace.

User Surveys

User surveys provide a wealth of information about how users use existing products, and ensure that our product meets our users' needs.

User Personas

I bring the data to life through the user personas, which help us to see from the user's point of view throughout the product development.

Phase 2: Information Architecture

Wireframes

Sketches and wireframes are vitally important for quickly creating and iterating on different layouts for information. The rough outlines and lack of color allow us to focus on how well the information is organized on the screen.

User Testing

Testing with users early and often takes the guesswork out of design. The users are always the best reference for how usable your product is.

Phase 3: Logo and Branding

Logo

I use the logo creation process to solidify the identity of the product. What do we do? What do we represent? What kind of people use our product? The answers to these questions help me to create a brand identity.

Style Guide

After defining the brand personality, creating the color palette, and finalizing the logo, I can create the first style guide. The style guide summarizes the brand identity, and acts as a guideline for the future design of the website, apps, and other media.
